
## Documents to Create

### 1. Project Charter

**ID:** b00de410-a788-4a5f-bc02-b74b8a8e9fca

**Description:** Formal document authorizing the project, defining its objectives, scope, stakeholders, and initial high-level budget. It outlines the project's alignment with strategic goals and provides the project manager with the authority to proceed. Includes initial success criteria and key assumptions.

**Responsible Role Type:** Project Manager

**Primary Template:** PMI Project Charter Template

**Steps:**

- Define project objectives and scope based on the project description.
- Identify key stakeholders and their roles.
- Establish initial budget and timeline estimates.
- Outline project governance and approval processes.
- Obtain sign-off from key stakeholders.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Investors

### 2. Geopolitical Risk Mitigation Strategy Framework

**ID:** 318588bf-ec9e-4449-9d95-a46c4b3070dd

**Description:** A high-level framework outlining the approach to minimizing potential disruptions and censorship issues arising from the film's sensitive themes. It defines risk assessment processes, mitigation strategies, and communication protocols. It addresses creative freedom vs. political palatability.

**Responsible Role Type:** Geopolitical Risk Assessor

**Steps:**

- Identify potential geopolitical risks based on the film's themes and setting.
- Assess the likelihood and impact of each risk.
- Develop mitigation strategies, including alternative narrative approaches and communication plans.
- Define a process for monitoring and responding to emerging risks.
- Incorporate legal counsel input.

**Approval Authorities:** Legal Counsel, Executive Producer

### 3. Narrative Innovation Strategy Plan

**ID:** a80d275e-3993-4ca4-8af4-66acb2629667

**Description:** A strategic plan outlining the approach to adapting the original film's story for a modern audience. It defines the degree of deviation from the original plot, particularly the ending, and outlines the creative vision for the remake. Addresses originality vs. familiarity.

**Responsible Role Type:** Screenwriter, Director

**Steps:**

- Analyze the original film's narrative and identify key themes and plot points.
- Develop alternative narrative approaches, including different endings and character arcs.
- Assess the potential impact of each approach on audience reception and critical acclaim.
- Define a clear creative vision for the remake.
- Obtain feedback from test audiences.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Director

### 4. Hong Kong Identity Amplification Strategy

**ID:** 2815d7a6-7aba-43b2-8026-f51c259eb804

**Description:** A strategic plan outlining the approach to integrating authentic Hong Kong elements into the film to resonate with local audiences and enhance the film's unique character. It defines the extent to which Hong Kong's culture, landmarks, and cinematic style are showcased. Addresses local authenticity vs. global accessibility.

**Responsible Role Type:** Hong Kong Cultural Liaison, Director

**Steps:**

- Identify key elements of Hong Kong culture, landmarks, and cinematic style.
- Develop strategies for integrating these elements into the film's narrative and visual design.
- Assess the potential impact of each element on local and international audiences.
- Collaborate with local filmmakers and crew to ensure authenticity.
- Obtain feedback from local communities.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Director

### 5. Initial Risk Register

**ID:** bd33a867-0df6-4039-84f6-1972d2a1b108

**Description:** A document identifying potential risks to the project, their likelihood and impact, and initial mitigation strategies. It covers regulatory, financial, technical, environmental, social, operational, supply chain, security, talent availability, market/competition, long-term sustainability, narrative execution, and currency fluctuation risks.

**Responsible Role Type:** Project Manager

**Primary Template:** PMI Risk Register Template

**Steps:**

- Identify potential risks based on the project description and assumptions.
- Assess the likelihood and impact of each risk.
- Develop initial mitigation strategies for each risk.
- Assign responsibility for monitoring and managing each risk.
- Review and update the risk register regularly.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Legal Counsel

### 6. Stakeholder Engagement Plan

**ID:** 96393ca1-97ca-496c-98b9-72ad3f989f29

**Description:** A plan outlining how the project will engage with key stakeholders, including the director, producer, screenwriter, lead actors, cinematographer, production designer, film editor, Hong Kong Film Development Fund, Propaganda Films / PolyGram (Universal), Hong Kong Film Censorship Authority, Hong Kong Tourism Board, local communities in Hong Kong, theatrical distributors, VOD platforms, streaming services, investors, film critics, and the target audience. It defines communication methods, frequency, and key messages.

**Responsible Role Type:** Marketing & Distribution Strategist

**Steps:**

- Identify key stakeholders and their interests.
- Define communication methods and frequency for each stakeholder group.
- Develop key messages for each stakeholder group.
- Establish a process for addressing stakeholder concerns.
- Review and update the stakeholder engagement plan regularly.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Project Manager

### 7. Communication Plan

**ID:** f2e340bb-560e-456c-836a-b890ad54c0ca

**Description:** A document outlining how project information will be communicated to stakeholders, including the frequency, method, and responsible party for each communication. It ensures timely and effective information dissemination.

**Responsible Role Type:** Project Manager

**Primary Template:** PMI Communication Plan Template

**Steps:**

- Identify stakeholders and their communication needs.
- Define communication channels and frequency.
- Assign responsibility for each communication.
- Establish a process for managing communication feedback.
- Review and update the communication plan regularly.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Marketing & Distribution Strategist

### 8. Change Management Plan

**ID:** d92f63b9-d2e9-4286-9f73-43ee6953ef0b

**Description:** A plan outlining the process for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget. It defines how changes will be identified, assessed, approved, and implemented. It ensures that changes are managed in a controlled and coordinated manner.

**Responsible Role Type:** Project Manager

**Primary Template:** PMI Change Management Plan Template

**Steps:**

- Define the change management process.
- Establish a change control board.
- Develop a change request form.
- Define the criteria for approving changes.
- Establish a process for implementing and tracking changes.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Financial Controller

### 9. High-Level Budget/Funding Framework

**ID:** de677ce3-9136-4f15-b386-6ed7c6008d07

**Description:** A high-level overview of the project's budget and funding sources, including estimated costs for production, marketing, and distribution. It outlines the funding strategy and identifies potential investors and funding opportunities. It provides a financial roadmap for the project.

**Responsible Role Type:** Financial Controller

**Steps:**

- Estimate the costs for each phase of the project.
- Identify potential funding sources, including investors, pre-sales, and grants.
- Develop a funding strategy and timeline.
- Create a financial model to project revenue and expenses.
- Review and update the budget/funding framework regularly.

**Approval Authorities:** Executive Producer, Investors
