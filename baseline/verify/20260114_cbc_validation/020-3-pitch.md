# Revolutionizing Space-Based Infrastructure with Coherent Beam Combining

## Project Overview

Imagine a future where space-based communication and energy transmission are not just dreams, but a reality. We are on the cusp of revolutionizing these fields, but a critical piece is missing: validated, reliable coherent beam combining in the harsh environment of space. This **$20 million project** will rigorously test and validate this technology, proving its viability and paving the way for groundbreaking advancements. We're not just building a system; we're building the future of space-based infrastructure!

## Goals and Objectives

The primary goal of this project is to **validate coherent beam combining** technology for space-based applications. This involves:

- Achieving the target **Strehl ratio** under stressed conditions.
- Demonstrating robust system performance in a simulated space environment.
- Validating our Thermal-Structural-Optical (TSO) scaling model.
- Establishing collaborative partnerships to accelerate technology development.

## Risks and Mitigation Strategies

We acknowledge the inherent risks associated with this project, including:

- Achieving the required **Strehl ratio** under stress.
- Control-structure interaction instabilities.
- Potential component delays.

Our mitigation strategies include:

- Robust modeling and simulation.
- Advanced control algorithms.
- Proactive supplier management.
- Comprehensive risk assessment throughout the project lifecycle.

## Metrics for Success

Beyond achieving the target **Strehl ratio** and wall-plug efficiency, success will be measured by:

- The accuracy of our Thermal-Structural-Optical (TSO) scaling model.
- The robustness of the system under various operating conditions.
- The number of collaborative partnerships established.

## Stakeholder Benefits

This project offers significant benefits to various stakeholders:

- Funding agencies will gain a validated technology for future space missions.
- Aerospace companies will have access to a proven beam-combining system for enhanced communication and power transmission capabilities.
- Research institutions will benefit from the data and knowledge generated by the project, advancing the field of space-based optics.
