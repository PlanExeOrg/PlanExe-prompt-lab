### 1. Project Steering Committee (PSC)

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Required for high-level strategic oversight, budget approval (especially budget sweeps or changes to the 4M DKK ceiling), and managing inter-departmental conflict regarding resource allocation between CSR, Logistics, and Finance. It ensures alignment with Arla's overall CSR strategy.

**Responsibilities:**

- Approve Go/No-Go decision for Q3 Nationwide Rollout.
- Review and authorize changes to the maximum donation outlay threshold (1.35M DKK ceiling).
- Provide strategic direction on addressing major setbacks affecting success criteria (e.g., if 40% recovery target looks unattainable by end of Q3).
- Oversight of overall project risk profile, focusing on reputational risk and major budget deviations.
- Final approval on external partnership contracts exceeding 500,000 DKK.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Finalize and ratify the Project Charter and Terms of Reference, defining financial thresholds.
- Elect the Committee Chair (likely a Senior VP from a relevant business unit).
- Establish formalized communication cadence with Arla Executive Leadership.

**Membership:**

- Head of Arla Supply Chain (Chair)
- Arla Foods Finance Director
- Head of Corporate Communications
- Arla Foundation Director (Non-voting Liaison)

**Decision Rights:** Authority over any decision impacting total budget variance exceeding 10% (400,000 DKK) or any change to the core success criteria (40% recovery target, 500k DKK donation goal). Approves final KPI reconciliation at campaign close.

**Decision Mechanism:** Simple majority vote by voting members. In case of a tie, the Chair casts the deciding vote, which must be ratified by the Finance Director within 48 hours.

**Meeting Cadence:** Monthly preceding the Q3/Q4 rollout; Bi-monthly during Q2 Pilot phase.

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Performance against 40% Recovery Target (Volume vs. Plan)
- Budget Burn Rate Review (Donation vs. Operational Overheads)
- Strategic Risk Assessment update (focus on Reputation/Financial Exposure)
- Approval requests for procurement variations (>500k DKK).

**Escalation Path:** Unresolved strategic disagreements or conflicts exceeding DKK 400,000 budget variance are escalated directly to the Arla Foods Executive Leadership Team.
### 2. Operational Program Management Office (OPMO)

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Necessary to manage the day-to-day execution of the complex, multi-site logistics (three hubs, multiple municipalities, numerous retailers) and monitor adherence to aggressive Q2/Q3 timelines. It bridges strategy (PSC) and tactical execution.

**Responsibilities:**

- Manage day-to-day stakeholder interactions (Municipalities, Retail Liaisons) ensuring SLA adherence (especially Risk 1 mitigation).
- Act as the primary conduit for Data Exchange protocols, ensuring monthly stock reports meet Procurement deadlines (Risk 7).
- Own the tracking of contamination rate (Risk 4) based on QA Hub reporting and trigger contingency ID strategy if necessary.
- Oversee deployment of physical materials (Signage, Bins) across all sites.
- Manage the DKK 5 subsidy reconciliation process for supermarkets (Risk 2 mitigation).

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Develop the Operations Manual incorporating all mitigation strategies (e.g., municipal staffing coefficients).
- Finalize KPI tracking suite (Dashboards) integrating data feeds from Hubs and Retail Liaisons.
- Establish conflict resolution escalation channel with the PSC.

**Membership:**

- Project Director/Manager (Chair)
- Logistics Lead (Overseeing 3 QA Hubs)
- Retail & Municipal Partnership Coordinator
- Finance Analyst (Budget monitoring below strategic thresholds)

**Decision Rights:** Authority over operational spending up to 150,000 DKK per month that does not impact the 4M DKK overall ceiling. Decides on minor deviations in marketing collateral placement (within pre-approved parameters) and logistical sequencing.

**Decision Mechanism:** Consensus among all members. If consensus fails, the Project Director makes the final decision. Escalation to PSC required if operational expenditure exceeds the 150k/month threshold.

**Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly during Q2/Q3 rollout; Weekly during Q2 Pilot phase.

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Status update on Municipal SLA compliance and training completion.
- QA Hub intake volumes, cleaning throughput, and contamination diagnostics.
- Review of stakeholder feedback channels (retail staff burden, public confusion).
- Preparation of necessary approvals/reports for PSC review.

**Escalation Path:** Issues requiring budget changes above 150,000 DKK per incident, disputes requiring formal partner renegotiation (SLAs), or failure to meet Q3 volume forecasts are immediately escalated to the Project Steering Committee (PSC).
### 3. Compliance and Environmental Assurance Group (CEAG)

**Rationale for Inclusion:** The project involves food-contact material reuse (HDPE plastics) and relies heavily on environmental claims (CO2 reduction). A dedicated body is essential to manage mandatory external regulatory clearances (Danish EPA) and internal ethical standards, mitigating significant legal and reputational risk (Assumptions 4 & 6).

**Responsibilities:**

- Obtain and maintain final Danish EPA certification for cleaning agents and the reuse process (Target: May 30th, 2026).
- Conduct mandatory internal holding point audit (July 10th) before any crate reintroduction into the supply chain.
- Oversee consultation process with external environmental bodies regarding CO2 calculation methodology.
- Ensure compliance with GDPR regarding public data/feedback handling, despite low-risk consumer interaction.
- Review compliance of the 'Ready-to-Deploy Stock Report' reconciliation process against new crate purchasing to validate climate benefit claims.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Secure commitment/retainer for an external environmental consultant to manage EPA submissions.
- Draft the formal compliance matrix linking internal audit checks to external regulatory requirements.
- Establish the internal hold-point checkpoint date (2026-07-10) within the project plan.

**Membership:**

- Arla Group Legal/Compliance Officer (Chair)
- Quality Assurance Manager (QA Hubs)
- Sustainability Lead (Internal Audit Function)
- External Environmental Consultant (Independent Assurance)

**Decision Rights:** Authority to halt the reintroduction of recovered crates into the primary supply chain if external EPA certification is not secured by the mandated hold date (2026-07-10), irrespective of volume recovered. Approval/rejection of cleaning processes.

**Decision Mechanism:** Unanimous agreement required on the continuation of the reuse pipeline, as public health and regulatory integrity override operational speed.

**Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly during Q1/Q2 (Certification Phase); Monthly during Q3/Q4 (Operational Phase).

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Status of external certification submissions and timelines (EPA).
- Review of contamination rates and QA Hub cleaning effectiveness.
- Audit of inventory reconciliation processes to confirm CO2 benefit realization.
- Review of advertising copy for environmental claims accuracy.

**Escalation Path:** Any unresolved dispute threatening the regulatory sign-off or material safety profile is escalated immediately to the Project Steering Committee (PSC) for intervention with Arla Executive Leadership.
