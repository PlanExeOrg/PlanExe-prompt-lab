
## Audit - Corruption Risks

- Kickbacks or bribery solicited by municipal waste authority staff to prioritize the acceptance of returned Arla crates at recycling stations (Genbrugsstationer) over standard waste, especially if performance metrics or spot-checks are lax.
- Nepotism or conflicts of interest influencing the awarding of the high-value 3rd-party logistics contracts required for material conveyance to the three centralized Quality Assessment (QA) Hubs.
- Misuse of the DKK 50 performance subsidy mechanism for supermarkets; store managers or personnel accepting the subsidy while failing to properly report returns or secure the dedicated drop-off space, effectively trading partial compliance for funds.
- Information misuse where personnel involved in the logistics chain (e.g., at the QA Hubs) leak planned crate restocking schedules or supply chain gaps to external parties for unauthorized gain.
- Trading favors with supermarket chain procurement teams to ensure their specific chain receives preferential treatment for collection times or logistical support in exchange for facilitating easy in-store drop-off procedures.

## Audit - Misallocation Risks

- Budgetary funds allocated for Logistics/QA contract management (1.8M DKK) being diverted to cover inflated or fraudulent transport costs to the centralized hubs due to weak oversight on distance/volume tracking (Risk 3).
- Misallocation of staff time at participating supermarkets, where staff spend excessive operational hours managing the crate drop-off area, resulting in unbudgeted labor costs billed back to Arla or covered through unauthorized operational overtime.
- Double spending on replacement crates if the monthly 'Ready-to-Deploy Stock Report' to Arla Procurement is delayed or inaccurate (post-September 2026), leading Arla to purchase new crates while recovered, cleaned stock sits unused.
- Improper routing of reusable crates: Crates deemed fit for reuse might be incorrectly logged as 'damaged for recycling' by overwhelmed local triage staff (especially at municipal sites), leading to material waste contrary to the CO2 goal.
- Misuse of the earmarked contingency budget (180,000 DKK identified for logistics overrun) being spent proactively on non-critical marketing activities rather than being reserved for the calculated risk of high recovery volume or logistical bottlenecks.

## Audit - Procedures

- Quarterly financial audits focused specifically on the Logistics/QA budget (45% allocation), requiring reconciliation of 3rd party transport invoices against confirmed daily intake volumes recorded at the three centralized hubs.
- Periodic (monthly during Q3/Q4 rollout) verification checks of the required 'Ready-to-Deploy Stock Report' data submissions to Arla Procurement against physical inventory counts at the QA hubs to ensure no double spending on new crates.
- Post-campaign external audit of the charitable outlay, confirming that the total donation payout (up to 1.35M DKK) aligns precisely with verified crate return receipts processed through authorized supermarket and municipal channels.
- Compliance check every two months on municipal station adherence to signed SLAs, verifying that the guaranteed minimum staff allocation coefficient (Expert Assumption 3) is maintained to support the 60% volume target.
- Process walkthrough audit of the identification protocols during the Q2 pilot phase, tracing 100 randomly selected returned crates from consumer drop-off through inspection and cleaning to verify the contamination rate (Risk 4) remains below sustainable thresholds.

## Audit - Transparency Measures

- Publish a quarterly 'Crate Recovery & Impact Report' summary, accessible via the Arla website, detailing DKK donated, crates recovered vs. target (108,000), and the estimated CO2 tonnage reduction achieved.
- Establish a public 'Stakeholder Compliance Dashboard' showing, at an aggregate level, the participation rates of the three supermarket chains and whether they are receiving or forfeiting the performance subsidy threshold.
- Publicly document the criteria and weighting used for selecting the three centralized QA Hub locations, making this information available to municipal partners to demonstrate fair logistical overhead distribution.
- Maintain and publish minutes (summarized for confidentiality) from the mandatory monthly synchronization meetings between QA Hub Logistics Lead and Arla Procurement, detailing stock levels and forecast alignment.
- Ensure proactive communication regarding the Danish EPA certification status for cleaning agents; publish the date of certification sign-off (target May 30th) to confirm the integrity of the reintroduction pipeline.
