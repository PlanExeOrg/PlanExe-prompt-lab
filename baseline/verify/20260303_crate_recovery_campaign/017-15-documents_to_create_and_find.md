- Search Danish university research repositories focusing on behavioral economics or consumer psychology.
- Review reports published by large consumer advocacy groups or the Arla Foundation's own prior evaluations.

**Access Difficulty**: Medium

**Essential Information**:

- Quantify the effectiveness ratio of a fixed 5 DKK monetary donation versus an equivalent tangible nudge ('one meal') in motivating Danish consumer participation in CSR activities.
- Identify the specific threshold (if any) where a small monetary incentive crosses from being negligible to being a significant behavioral driver for Danish consumers concerning logistics participation.
- List consumer compliance rates associated with both monetary and tangible nudges based on existing field or academic reports.
- Detail any data on the comparative complexity or friction associated with redeeming a monetary incentive versus acknowledging a tangible benefit realization (e.g., donation confirmation).

**Risks of Poor Quality**:

- Using outdated or irrelevant economic context (pre-inflation analysis) will lead to an inaccurate assumption that the 5 DKK incentive is sufficient, causing failure to meet the critical 40% volume target.
- If the report is biased (e.g., solely NGO-driven without accounting for purely transactional incentives), the resulting calibration of Lever 6 may misallocate resources, either over-spending on incentives or under-performing on volume.
- If the report does not focus on logistics/return participation specifically, the data may not translate to the physical inconvenience factor inherent in returning a milk crate.

**Worst Case Scenario**: Selecting the wrong incentive structure based on flawed external data will directly cause the 40% recovery volume goal (108,000 crates) to be missed, leading to failure in the secondary CO2 reduction metric and jeopardizing the viability of the entire 2026 operational plan.

**Best Case Scenario**: High-quality data validates the 5 DKK fixed monetary incentive, allowing the project team to confidently reject the costlier, more complex tiered/matching incentive options, thereby preserving the 1.35M DKK donation budget ceiling and freeing up the Marketing budget for necessary Risk 6 mitigation.

**Fallback Alternative Approaches**:

- Immediately trigger the contingency plan identified in the expert review: Assume DKK 5 effectiveness is low and provisionally reallocate up to 200,000 DKK from Marketing to create an immediate budget buffer for an emergency 30% incentive increase (DKK 6.50) if Q3 volume targets are missed after 6 weeks.
- Commission a rapid, low-cost consumer survey (n=300) focused solely on the perceived effort/reward ratio of physically returning a large item versus receiving a 5 DKK digital confirmation.
- Defer the final decision on Incentive Structure Calibration (Lever 6) until after the Q2 pilot completion, basing the decision strictly on early pilot return volume against expected benchmarks, regardless of external literature.
