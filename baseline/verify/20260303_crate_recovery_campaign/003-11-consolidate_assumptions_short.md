# Identify Risks
## Risk 1 - Operational / Logistics

- Reliance on municipal recycling stations (60% volume). Capacity shortages risk overwhelmed sites, consumer frustration, lost crates.
- Impact: 10% volume loss (16,200 crates) delays 40% target by 3-5 weeks; adds 50,000 - 100,000 DKK remedial cost.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: High
- Action: Establish formal SLAs with municipal authorities (capacity, staffing). Pre-deploy high-visibility temporary signage and robust collection bins.

## Risk 2 - Stakeholder Integration (Supermarkets)

- Reliance on performance-based subsidies (50 DKK/week) for >100 returns/month. Low-performing stores may offer minimal/non-compliant service.
- Impact: Low compliance (30-40% stores) misses target by 15,000-30,000 crates; rework costs 100,000 DKK in ad-hoc field operations.
- Likelihood: High
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Implement low-level, universal requirement (e.g., 200 DKK monthly fee) for basic compliance (signage/space); reserve 50 DKK subsidy as a bonus.

## Risk 3 - Logistics & Quality Assurance

- Centralized temporary quality assessment hubs. Inefficient logistics or centralization too far from return areas escalates cleaning transport costs, straining 4M DKK budget.
- Impact: Transport costs 20% higher than modeled consume up to 400,000 DKK of non-donation budget, requiring marketing spend or QA staff reduction.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: High
- Action: Conduct pre-campaign logistical study using simulated return densities to finalize three hub locations, minimizing average distance. Include volume/distance-based cost caps in 3rd party logistics contracts.

## Risk 4 - Technical / Identification

- Identification relies on social media comparative visual guides, depending on consumer memory to distinguish similar crates.
- Impact: Contamination >15% (vs. 5% sustainable) strains cleaning, causes quality rejections, undermining CO2 metric.
- Likelihood: High
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Develop contingency physical ID strategy (low-cost, non-permanent feature like barcode or molded marker) for rapid communication if social media fails during pilot.

## Risk 5 - Financial / Budget Overrun

- High success (>270,000 returns) exceeds budgeted donation outlay (1.35M DKK contingency), straining operational budget (max 4M DKK).
- Impact: 50,000 extra crates increase donation by 250,000 DKK, requiring marketing budget cuts or delayed service securing.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Formalize 'Return Cap Threshold' with Arla Foundation/Finance. Define donation commitment only to annual loss volume (270,000); returns above revert to standard pathway.

## Risk 6 - Social / Public Acceptance

- Marketing uses provocative narrative ('Garden Furniture is Costing Denmark CO2'), risking alienation of core family consumers if perceived as guilt-tripping.
- Impact: Negative backlash suppresses returns by 10-20% (10,800-21,600 crates) during crucial Q3/Q4 rollout, failing primary success criteria.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Pre-test provocative messaging with target demographics via Arla Foundation channels. Balance negative messaging with positive calls-to-action for child nutrition education.

## Risk 7 - Supply Chain / Integration

- Reintroducing cleaned crates requires perfect timing with new 2027 crate orders. Slow central hub processing results in pre-ordering replacement stock, causing overflow or unnecessary production.
- Impact: 20% of recovered crates arriving 6 weeks late erodes targeted CO2 reduction benefit for that year.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Low
- Action: Establish mandatory, clear feedback loops between Logistics & QA Management and Production Planning, requiring monthly reconciliation reports on redeployment-ready volume versus projected demand.

## Risk summary
Project is low-risk, but 'Builder' strategy entails operational complexity. Critical risks: Logistical Capacity (Municipal Stations impacting high-volume goal); Stakeholder Compliance (Supermarkets undermining returns); and Identification Accuracy (impacting QA hub efficiency/cost). Focus mitigation on firm municipal agreements and establishing basic supermarket compliance floors. Clear ID reduces contamination, easing QA load and supporting financial ceiling.

## Question 2 - Q3 Launch Logistical Milestones (Post Q2 Pilot)

- Assumptions:

    - Q2 pilot completion: 2026-06-30.
    - Hub location finalization and sanitation procurement: due 2026-07-15.
    - Municipal SLAs (Risk 1) formally signed/ratified: due 2026-07-31.

- Assessments: Timeline Adherence Checkpoint

    - Timeline is tight; only 4 weeks for high-stakes municipal SLAs post-pilot.
    - Risk: Delay of municipal station training/signage (Risk 1 mitigation) past July 31st jeopardizes 60% volume throughput and target recovery (108,000 crates).
    - Opportunity: Early hub setup allows front-loading Q3 marketing spend.

## Question 3 - Personnel Requirements for Three QA Hubs (Q3-Q4 2026)

- Assumptions:

    - Total operational roles: 27 FTE across 3 hubs.
    - Staffing per hub: 1 Site Manager (Arla), 2 Logistics Coordinators (contractors), 6 Cleaning/Sorting Operatives (contracted labor).

- Assessments: Resource Allocation & Staffing Risk

    - Risk: Understaffing cleaning crews risks backlog if contamination (Risk 4) is high, impacting the 1.8M DKK logistics budget.
    - Benefit: Centralization allows specialized training, projecting 98% reintroduction rate.
    - Risk: Failure to secure specialized cleaning/sorting contractors by May 2026 causes operational lag post-pilot.

## Question 4 - Regulatory Compliance for Crate Handling

- Assumptions:

    - Arla Legal/Compliance conducts Q2 2026 review on cleaning agents for EPA standards (food-contact plastic reuse).
    - Standard waste transport waiver/permit secured for defined routes.

- Assessments: Governance and Regulatory Compliance

    - Risk: Uncertified cleaning protocols could taint crates, invalidating CO2 benefit and incurring fines.
    - Opportunity: Proactive Q2 certification can be leveraged in marketing.
    - Risk: Failure to secure cross-regional transport permits halts logistics; remediation estimated at 4-6 weeks.

## Question 5 - Operational Procedures for Municipal Staff Triage Training

- Assumptions:

    - Training: Standardized one-day on-site workshop plus laminated quick-reference guides at intake areas (Q3-Q4 2026).

- Assessments: Safety and Contamination Risk Management at Drop-off Site

    - Risk: Inadequate training at high-volume sites leads to poor sorting (contamination) or unsafe handling.
    - Requirement: Training must cover safe handling of potentially heavier crates due to municipal focus (Risk 1).
    - Benefit: Well-trained staff reduces source contamination, easing hub burden (Risk 3).
    - Mitigation: Arla must cover outsourced training service costs.

## Question 6 - Consultation with External Environmental Bodies

- Assumptions:

    - Primary municipal partnerships cover end-of-life routing.
    - Consultation with Danish Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) regarding material flow tracking is mandatory.

- Assessments: External Environmental Stewardship Engagement

    - Risk: Non-engagement risks negative press positioning campaign as purely self-serving PR.
    - Opportunity: Early EPA engagement can lead to official endorsement of CO2 savings calculation, boosting credibility.
    - Requirement: Consultation costs budgeted within the 850k DKK marketing/overhead pillar.

## Question 7 - Communication Strategy vs. Retailer Relationships

- Assumptions:

    - Supermarket collateral (signage/posters) requires pre-approval 30 days prior by retail liaisons.
    - Messaging focus: Charitable donation (Arla Foundation) over potentially accusatory environmental tone (Risk 6 mitigation).

- Assessments: Stakeholder Synergy and Partnership Management

    - Risk: Provocative marketing perceived by store managers as antagonistic (Risk 2) will reduce retail compliance.
    - Requirement: Messaging must emphasize charity to maintain harmonious long-term relationships.
    - Benefit: Pre-approval streamlines deployment and builds trust with retailers.

## Question 8 - Data Exchange for 2027 Production Forecasting

- Assumptions:

    - Mandatory monthly 'Ready-to-Deploy Stock Report' generated by Central Hub Logistics Lead.
    - Report submission to Arla Procurement by the 5th day of every month, starting September 2026.

- Assessments: Operational Systems Integration for Future Planning

    - Risk: Failure to establish formal, timely data exchange (Risk 7) risks unnecessary new-crate purchasing, contradicting CO2 reduction goals.
    - Requirement: Formal monthly reporting mechanism established.
    - Opportunity: Successful integration can blueprint tracking for other Arla packaging categories.

# Review Assumptions
## Domain of the expert reviewer
Project Success & Risk Planning for Complex Physical and Stakeholder Initiatives

## Domain-specific considerations

- Balancing operational logistics (reverse supply chain) with public behavior modification (incentives/narrative).
- Managing dependencies across highly divergent stakeholders: high-street retail partners and municipal government entities.
- Financial viability of high-volume, low-margin recovery operations under a fixed budget ceiling.
- Regulatory compliance for cleaning and reuse of food-contact materials at an industrial scale.

## Issue 1 - Critical Missing Assumption: Stability of Consumer Incentive Value (DKK 5)

- Plan assumes fixed DKK 5 donation meets 40% recovery target, linked strongly to 'low volume, high visibility' supermarket returns.
- Missing assumption: Impact of inflation/economic shifts on *perceived* value of DKK 5 relative to consumer time/effort. Failure impacts 60% volume target at municipal stations.

Recommendation: Assume mandatory sensitivity trigger: If Q3 returns < 15% of expected monthly volume after 6 weeks, trigger immediate review of Incentive Structure Calibration (Lever 6). Pre-approve budget sweep up to DKK 200,000 from Marketing/Overhead (total 850k DKK) to fund emergency 30% incentive increase (DKK 5 to DKK 6.50) for last month of Q4, contingent on hitting volume gaps.

Sensitivity: If DKK 5 perceived value is 80% (i.e., DKK 4), recovery volume drops 15-25% (40,500 to 67,500 fewer crates). This reduces CO2 reduction KPI by 15-25% and requires 5-10% marketing budget increase, failing ROI.

## Issue 2 - Critical Missing Assumption: Regulatory Approval Timeline for Cleaning Agents & Reintroduction

- Assumption 4 covers *internal* legal review by Q2 2026 but omits crucial external regulatory approval, certification, and third-party testing by Danish EPA for reusing food-contact plastics. External validation is prerequisite for Arla reintroduction (failing Risk 7).

Recommendation: Formally assume external certification requires minimum of 8 weeks post-internal review confirmation (Q2 2026). Quality Assurance Modality sign-off must be no later than 2026-05-30. Mandate shifting procurement of specific cleaning chemicals from logistics budget (1.8M DKK) to Q1 2026 for lead time.

Sensitivity: Baseline 8-week delay pushes validated reintroduction to late Q3, missing Q3 national launch (violates Assumption 2), delaying ROI by 2-3 months. Cost of delay: DKK 20,000 per week lost utilization across three hubs.

## Issue 3 - Under-Explored Assumption: Operational Capacity of Municipal Stations vs. 60% Load

- 'Builder' path relies on municipal stations handling 60% of volume (162,000 crates). Municipal facilities are for mixed-waste, not high-velocity reverse logistics.
- Assumption 5 details training, but no guaranteed assumption for storage, security, or time commitment from municipal staff for Arla returns vs. easier subsidized supermarket returns.

Recommendation: Introduce dual-channel compliance assumption: Municipal SLAs (Risk 1 mitigation) must include guaranteed minimum staff allocation coefficient (e.g., 1 FTE per 10,000 expected weekly returns for Arla supervision/sorting). For 40% supermarket channel, mandate fixed participation admin fee (Risk 2 mitigation) to cover basic in-store setup, ensuring compliance floor.

Sensitivity: If municipal stations only handle 40% instead of 60% (20% shortfall, ~54,000 crates), the surplus must be stored insecurely or absorbed by supermarkets. This requires mobilizing costly emergency mobile hubs (Option 3), potentially increasing Logistics Budget (1.8M DKK) by DKK 300,000 - 500,000, or delaying recovery by 6-8 weeks.

## Review conclusion
Plan is strategically sound ('Builder' path), but relies on optimistic assumptions regarding external regulation timelines and non-primary stakeholder commitment (municipalities). Critical failures: (1) Fixed DKK 5 incentive stability, (2) omission of external regulatory approval time, and (3) underestimation of guaranteed municipal capacity/dedication. Recommendations focus on securing contingency for incentive increases, front-loading regulatory engagement (target May 2026), and enforcing firm staffing coefficients in municipal SLAs to safeguard 60% volume allocation.
