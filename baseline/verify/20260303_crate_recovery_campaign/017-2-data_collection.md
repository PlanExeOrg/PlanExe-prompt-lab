## 1. Municipal Station Capacity and SLA Validation

Municipal stations handle 60% of the target volume. Underestimation of staffing requirements or unexpected gate fees directly causes budget overrun (Risk 3) and failure to meet volume goals (Risk 1), making this the highest operational dependency.

### Data to Collect

- Formal Service Level Agreement (SLA) documents signed with key municipal waste authorities.
- Guaranteed minimum staff allocation coefficient (FTE hours per expected Arla crate volume) at municipal stations.
- Agreed penalty/remediation cost structure for municipal capacity shortfalls.
- Confirmed classification of returned Arla crates (household vs. commercial waste) by relevant municipal/Danish authorities.

### Simulation Steps

- Use spreadsheet modeling (e.g., Microsoft Excel/Google Sheets) to map the 60% volume target against the guaranteed contracted staff hours, identifying potential staffing deficit hours during peak return times.
- Model the financial impact of the predicted gate/waste tax fees (assume 1.5 DKK/kg for calculation) against the total Logistics Budget (1.8M DKK) based on crate weight projections.

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult the Municipal Logistics Negotiator (Expert 5) to review draft SLAs, specifically focusing on the enforceability of staffing coefficients and penalty clauses for Q3 deployment.
- Consult the Circular Economy Consultant (Expert 1) to audit SLAs against current Danish EPR and waste tax regulations to confirm commercial waste classification risks are mitigated.

### Responsible Parties

- Stakeholder Integration & Municipal Relations Manager
- Financial Controller & Budget Steward
- Circular Economy Consultant

### Assumptions

- **High:** Municipalities will agree to specific, legally binding staffing coefficients as part of the SLA based on goodwill and narrative framing alone.
- **Medium:** The assumed cost for unexpected municipal waste gate fees (1.5 DKK/kg) is accurate for the Danish waste management sector.

### SMART Validation Objective

Formal, legally binding SLAs covering guaranteed staffing coefficients and penalty clauses for the top 5 municipalities (covering >70% of expected 60% volume) must be signed and ratified by 2026-07-31, verified by internal Legal Counsel.

### Notes

- This directly addresses Missing Information #3 and Risk 1.
- The regulatory confirmation (Expert 1) must precede final SLA signing.

- Formal documented 'Return Cap Threshold' agreement signed by Finance/Arla Foundation specifying when the 1.35M DKK donation budget is formally closed or requires a budget sweep authorization.

## 4. Supermarket Compliance Floor & Subsidy Structure

Supermarket staff compliance (Risk 2) is crucial for the high-visibility, low-volume channel. Moving from a pure incentive to a compliance floor ensures basic operational presence, safeguarding the low-risk mandate.

- Data from the Q2 pilot showing the correlation between the performance-based subsidy (50 DKK/week for >100 returns) and reported staff adherence scores.
- Escalation path documentation specifying actions taken against non-compliant store managers operating below the Compliance Floor standard.

### Expert Validation Steps

### Responsible Parties

## Summary

The project plan focuses on the 'Builder' scenario, balancing high-capacity municipal returns (60%) with manageable retail integration, while urgently addressing two critical compliance dependencies: regulatory clearance for food-contact reuse and firm Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with municipalities.

**Critical Next Steps:**
1. **Regulatory Frontloading:** The Regulatory & Compliance Assessor must immediately secure pre-approved cleaning agents and submit documentation to the Danish EPA, targeting certification sign-off by 2026-07-15 (Data Item 2). This is the highest risk timeline issue.
2. **Municipal Contracts:** The Stakeholder Manager must begin formal SLA negotiation with municipalities, focusing immediately on securing guaranteed staffing coefficients to safeguard the 60% volume allocation, with final treaty ratification due 2026-07-31 (Data Item 1).
3. **Digital Uplift:** The Incentive Specialist and PR Architect must accelerate the design and pilot-readiness of the QR-code 'Killer Application' by the end of Q2 2026, as it is the key leverage point to drive organic impressions and compensate for the static 5 DKK incentive (Data Item 5).
4. **Financial Preparation:** The Financial Controller must finalize the documented 'Return Cap Threshold' for the 1.35M DKK donation budget, clarifying the trigger and authorization for budget sweeps related to incentive adjustments (Data Item 3).
