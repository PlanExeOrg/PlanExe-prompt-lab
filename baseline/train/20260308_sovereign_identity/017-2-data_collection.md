## 1. Financial Sustainability and Grant Opportunities

Ensuring financial sustainability is crucial for the project's long-term viability. Understanding grant opportunities and managing currency risks are essential for effective budget management.

### Data to Collect

- Detailed budget breakdown with specific cost items.
- List of potential EU and private grant opportunities.
- Grant application requirements and deadlines.
- DKK-specific accounting standards for grant management.
- Currency exchange rate forecasts and historical data.

### Simulation Steps

- Use financial modeling software (e.g., Excel, Google Sheets) to create a detailed budget breakdown.
- Utilize online grant databases (e.g., EU Funding & Tenders Portal) to identify potential grant opportunities.
- Simulate currency exchange rate fluctuations using historical data from financial websites (e.g., Yahoo Finance, Google Finance).

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with a financial auditor experienced in grant accounting in Denmark.
- Engage a grant writer specializing in EU and private funding.
- Seek advice from a financial advisor specializing in currency risk management.

### Responsible Parties

- Policy and Public-Affairs Coordinator
- Lead Researcher
- Project Manager

### Assumptions

- **High:** EU and private grants will be available to supplement the 10.5 million DKK budget.
- **Medium:** The project team has sufficient expertise in grant accounting and financial management.

### SMART Validation Objective

Secure at least 5 million DKK in additional funding from EU or private grants by 2027-03-08 to ensure long-term financial sustainability.

### Notes

- Uncertainty exists regarding the availability and competitiveness of grant opportunities.
- Risk of currency fluctuations impacting the project budget.
- Missing a detailed financial sustainability plan.


## 2. Technical Definitions of Platform Neutrality and Fallback Access

Clear and precise technical definitions are essential for guiding the project's technical demonstrators and procurement influence efforts. Without these definitions, the project risks developing solutions that do not address the core problem of platform dependency.

### Data to Collect

- Specific aspects of Apple and Google platforms considered unacceptable dependencies (e.g., app stores, OS-level APIs).
- Technical requirements for fallback access paths (e.g., authentication methods, security protocols).
- Analysis of AltID's technical requirements and constraints.
- Examples of procurement language promoting platform neutrality.

### Simulation Steps

- Research existing definitions of platform neutrality and related concepts from organizations like the W3C and IETF.
- Simulate different fallback authentication scenarios using software tools like OpenSSL and WebAuthn libraries.
- Analyze AltID documentation and publicly available information to understand its technical architecture.

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with a mobile security expert and a regulatory compliance specialist.
- Engage a web authentication expert and a hardware security module (HSM) specialist.
- Consult with Digitaliseringsstyrelsen and the MitID operator.
- Seek advice from a procurement lawyer and a technical expert.

### Responsible Parties

- Lead Researcher
- Technical Lead
- Regulatory and Compliance Specialist
- Policy and Public-Affairs Coordinator

### Assumptions

- **High:** The project team has sufficient technical expertise to define platform neutrality and fallback access in a legally and technically sound manner.
- **Medium:** Digitaliseringsstyrelsen and the MitID operator will be willing to share information about AltID's technical requirements and constraints.

### SMART Validation Objective

Develop and publish precise technical definitions for 'platform neutrality' and 'fallback authentication' by 2026-06-01, specifying technical characteristics, security requirements, and acceptable technologies.

### Notes

- Risk of ambiguity and misinterpretation of key concepts.
- Potential for vendor circumvention of platform-neutrality requirements.
- Missing a detailed analysis of AltID's technical requirements and constraints.

## 4. Alternative Scenarios and Intervention Points

Over-reliance on eIDAS2 and AltID as primary intervention points carries significant risk. Developing alternative scenarios and intervention points is crucial for ensuring the project's impact even if these avenues prove less receptive than anticipated.

### Data to Collect

- Potential fallback positions if AltID procurement proves resistant to platform-neutrality requirements.
- Alternative legal and regulatory pathways for promoting platform neutrality.
- Actions Denmark can take unilaterally to advance digital sovereignty.
- Specific technical standards or open-source initiatives that Denmark could champion independently.

### Simulation Steps

- Conduct scenario planning workshops to explore alternative intervention points.
- Research existing legal and regulatory frameworks related to digital identity and procurement.
- Analyze successful examples of countries promoting digital sovereignty through independent initiatives.

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with experts in EU digital policy and Danish procurement law.
- Engage with policymakers and stakeholders to assess the feasibility of alternative scenarios.
- Seek advice from legal counsel on alternative legal and regulatory pathways.

### Responsible Parties

- Policy and Public-Affairs Coordinator
- Lead Researcher
- Regulatory and Compliance Specialist

### Assumptions

- **Medium:** Experts in EU digital policy and Danish procurement law will be available to provide guidance on alternative scenarios.
- **Low:** Policymakers and stakeholders will be open to considering alternative scenarios for promoting platform neutrality.

### SMART Validation Objective

Develop and document at least three alternative scenarios and intervention points for promoting platform neutrality by 2026-06-01, including fallback positions if AltID procurement proves resistant to platform-neutrality requirements.

### Notes

- Risk of limited impact if AltID procurement or eIDAS2 developments do not align with the project's goals.
- Potential for wasted resources and failure to achieve the desired outcome.
- Missing a robust contingency plan beyond influencing AltID and eIDAS2.


## 5. Technical Standards and Certification

eIDAS2 relies heavily on standards, and without a clear strategy for engaging with the standardization process, the project's impact on EU-level policy will be limited. The project needs to consider how platform-neutral solutions can be certified under eIDAS, particularly concerning assurance levels and conformity assessment.

### Data to Collect

- Relevant eIDAS-related standards and certification schemes.
- Specific standards bodies (e.g., ETSI, CEN/CENELEC) and technical working groups.
- Requirements for certifying platform-neutral solutions under eIDAS.
- Plan for engaging with standards bodies, including attending meetings and submitting proposals.

### Simulation Steps

- Research existing eIDAS-related standards and certification schemes using online databases and resources.
- Identify relevant standards bodies and technical working groups through online searches and industry contacts.
- Analyze eIDAS documentation and guidelines to understand the requirements for certification.

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with experts in eIDAS certification and conformity assessment.
- Engage with representatives from relevant standards bodies and technical working groups.
- Seek advice from legal counsel on the legal and regulatory aspects of certification.

### Responsible Parties

- Lead Researcher
- Technical Lead
- Regulatory and Compliance Specialist
- Policy and Public-Affairs Coordinator

### Assumptions

- **Medium:** Experts in eIDAS certification and conformity assessment will be available to provide guidance on certification requirements.
- **Low:** Relevant standards bodies and technical working groups will be receptive to the project's proposals for platform-neutral solutions.

### SMART Validation Objective

Develop a detailed plan for engaging with relevant standards bodies (e.g., ETSI, CEN/CENELEC) by 2026-09-01, including attending meetings, submitting proposals, and participating in working groups, and identify the requirements for certifying platform-neutral solutions under eIDAS.

### Notes

- Risk of limited impact on EU-level policy without a clear strategy for engaging with the standardization process.
- Potential for platform-neutral solutions not being certifiable under eIDAS.
- Insufficient focus on technical standards and certification.

## Summary

This project plan outlines the data collection and validation activities necessary to establish the technical, regulatory, and political basis for platform-neutral access requirements and a certified fallback authentication path for Danish digital identity. The plan focuses on addressing key risks and uncertainties identified in the project's initial assessment, including financial sustainability, technical definitions, security strategy, alternative scenarios, and technical standards. Expert validation is a critical component of this plan, ensuring that the project's assumptions and recommendations are sound and aligned with best practices.
