**Goal Statement:** Establish the technical, regulatory, and political basis for formal platform-neutral access requirements and a certified fallback authentication path for Danish digital identity within 24 months.

## SMART Criteria

- **Specific:** The goal is to create a foundation for platform-neutral access requirements and a certified fallback authentication path for Danish digital identity.
- **Measurable:** Success will be measured by the publication of a feasibility report, delivery of two working demonstrators, a formal written response from Digitaliseringsstyrelsen, and inclusion of platform-neutrality language in at least one AltID-related document.
- **Achievable:** The goal is achievable by focusing on influencing AltID and related procurement decisions, rather than attempting to replace MitID directly, and by building a coalition of support.
- **Relevant:** This goal is relevant because it addresses the Danish public-sector digital strategy's call for reduced dependence on a small number of large foreign technology suppliers and for stronger digital sovereignty.
- **Time-bound:** The project is to be completed within 24 months.

## Dependencies

- Secure funding for each phase of the project.
- Establish a physical base in Copenhagen.
- Build a coalition of support spanning civil-society organisations, privacy advocates, academic researchers, security experts, contingency-planning stakeholders, and sympathetic members of Folketinget's digital policy committee.

## Resources Required

- Mobile security and authentication protocols expertise
- Regulatory and compliance specialist familiar with Danish and EU digital identity frameworks
- Policy and public-affairs coordinator experienced in Danish digital governance and procurement processes
- Technical lead for the demonstrator work
- Engineer or contractor covering web authentication, hardware-token workflows, or Linux/GrapheneOS-related implementation
- Access-controlled workspace appropriate for security-sensitive digital identity research
- Budget for institutional overhead
- Budget for external security review
- Budget for limited legal/procurement support

## Risk Assessment and Mitigation Strategies


### Key Risks

- Delays in obtaining permits/approvals.
- Demonstrators difficult to implement. Dependence on specific platforms.
- Cost overruns. Budget gated across phases.
- Lack of buy-in from stakeholders. Resistance from vendors.
- Difficulty securing physical base in Copenhagen.
- Vulnerabilities in demonstrators.
- Complexity of integrating with MitID.

### Diverse Risks

- Financial risks
- Social risks
- Technical risks
- Operational risks
- Security risks
- Integration risks

### Mitigation Plans

- Conduct due diligence in Phase 1. Engage authorities. Develop contingency plans.
- Maintain a lean scope. Implement early prototyping. Ensure sufficient expertise.
- Implement budget tracking. Establish clear phase gate criteria. Maintain a contingency fund.
- Engage stakeholders proactively. Frame the issue strategically. Build a strong coalition.
- Search for a physical base early in Phase 1. Explore multiple options. Negotiate lease terms.
- Implement robust security measures. Conduct regular security reviews. Engage external experts.
- Focus on AltID. Develop clear guidelines. Conduct thorough testing. Engage the MitID operator.
