# Securing Denmark's Digital Sovereignty

## Project Overview
This project envisions a Denmark where access to public services is not controlled by **Silicon Valley giants**, but instead relies on a resilient, secure, and nationally controlled digital identity. We aim to establish the technical, regulatory, and political foundation for true digital sovereignty.

## Goals and Objectives
Our primary goal is to champion **platform-neutral access** and establish a certified fallback authentication path for Danish digital identity. This will ensure:

- Continuity of service.
- Reduced dependence on foreign technology.
- Strengthened national digital future.

## Risks and Mitigation Strategies
We acknowledge potential challenges, including:

- Delays in approvals.
- Technical difficulties in demonstrator implementation.
- Resistance from incumbent vendors.

Our mitigation strategies include:

- Proactive engagement with authorities.
- A lean project scope with early prototyping.
- Building a strong coalition of support.
- Detailed risk assessment and mitigation plan, including budget tracking, security reviews, and contingency funds.

## Metrics for Success
Success will be measured by:

- Increased public awareness and discussion of digital sovereignty in Denmark.
- Active participation and contributions from a diverse range of stakeholders.
- Measurable improvements in the security and resilience of Danish digital identity infrastructure.
- Positive feedback from end-users regarding the usability and accessibility of platform-neutral solutions.

## Stakeholder Benefits

- **Policymakers:** Gain a stronger, more secure digital infrastructure and increased national control.
- **Citizens:** Benefit from enhanced privacy and reduced reliance on foreign tech companies.
- **Civil society organizations:** Gain a platform to advocate for digital rights and open-source solutions.
- **Investors:** Access a growing market for digital sovereignty technologies and contribute to a more secure and independent digital future for Denmark.

## Ethical Considerations
We are committed to ethical development and deployment, prioritizing:

- User privacy.
- Accessibility for all citizens.
- Transparency in our processes.
- Adherence to GDPR and eIDAS regulations.
- Regular ethical reviews.

## Collaboration Opportunities
We actively seek collaboration through:

- Technical partnerships.
- Policy advocacy.
- Research collaborations.
- Community engagement.
- Contributions from open-source developers and security experts.

## Long-term Vision
Our long-term vision is a Denmark where digital identity is truly sovereign, empowering citizens and fostering **innovation**. We envision a future where platform neutrality is the norm, and Denmark leads in shaping European digital identity standards, creating a more resilient, secure, and democratic digital future.
