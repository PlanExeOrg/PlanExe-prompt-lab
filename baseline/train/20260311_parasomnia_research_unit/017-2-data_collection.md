## 1. Recruitment Channel Strategy

This data is critical to understand the effectiveness of different recruitment channels and their impact on participant demographics and enrollment rates.

### Data to Collect

- Number of participants enrolled from each recruitment channel
- Proportion of eligible participants from each channel
- Cost per enrolled participant

### Simulation Steps

- Use online survey tools (e.g., SurveyMonkey) to gather preliminary interest from potential participants
- Analyze historical data from previous studies to estimate enrollment rates from different channels

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with Dr. Erika Schmidt, Principal Investigator, for insights on recruitment strategies
- Engage with the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Schlafforschung und Schlafmedizin for best practices in recruitment

### Responsible Parties

- Study Coordinator
- Principal Investigator

### Assumptions

- **High:** Participants will respond positively to recruitment efforts.
- **Medium:** Recruitment channels will yield a diverse participant pool.

### SMART Validation Objective

By the end of Month 6, achieve a minimum of 50 enrolled participants with at least 30% from diverse recruitment channels, validated through participant records.

### Notes

- Consider potential biases in self-selection from online channels.


## 2. Recruitment Stringency Strategy

Understanding the impact of recruitment stringency on sample size and event yield is essential for ensuring robust study outcomes.

### Data to Collect

- Proportion of participants meeting stringent criteria
- Number of parasomnia events captured
- Sample size of participants

### Simulation Steps

- Run simulations using statistical software (e.g., R) to model the impact of different stringency levels on sample size and event capture rates
- Conduct focus groups with sleep specialists to gather qualitative data on screening criteria

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with a neurologist specializing in sleep disorders to validate screening criteria
- Engage with a biostatistician to assess the statistical power of the proposed sample sizes

### Responsible Parties

- Principal Investigator
- Postdoctoral Researchers

### Assumptions

- **High:** Stricter criteria will lead to fewer but more relevant participants.
- **Medium:** The sample size will be sufficient to capture meaningful data.

### SMART Validation Objective

By Month 12, validate that at least 70% of participants meet the stringent criteria while capturing a minimum of 100 parasomnia events.

### Notes

- Monitor for potential ethical concerns regarding participant exclusion.


## 3. Data Acquisition Intensity Strategy

This data is crucial for balancing data richness with participant burden, directly impacting the study's validity.

### Data to Collect

- Signal-to-noise ratio of collected data
- Participant dropout rates
- Completeness of data records

### Simulation Steps

- Use data analysis software (e.g., MATLAB) to simulate different data acquisition intensities and their effects on data quality
- Conduct pilot studies to assess participant comfort with various monitoring intensities

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with sleep technologists to evaluate the effectiveness of different monitoring techniques
- Engage with a clinical psychologist to assess participant comfort levels

### Responsible Parties

- Data Engineer
- Research Technicians

### Assumptions

- **High:** Higher data acquisition intensity will yield better data quality.
- **Medium:** Participants will tolerate increased monitoring without significant dropout.

### SMART Validation Objective

By Month 18, achieve a signal-to-noise ratio of at least 5:1 with a participant dropout rate below 10%.

### Notes

- Consider participant feedback on comfort during data collection.


## 4. Staffing Coverage Model

This data is essential for ensuring participant safety and data quality, impacting operational efficiency.

### Data to Collect

- Speed of response to safety incidents
- Completeness of initial data annotation

### Simulation Steps

- Model staffing scenarios using project management software (e.g., Microsoft Project) to assess coverage needs
- Analyze historical incident response times to identify optimal staffing levels

### Expert Validation Steps

- Consult with a clinical trial manager to validate staffing models
- Engage with safety experts to assess incident response protocols

### Responsible Parties

- Facility and Safety Manager
- Principal Investigator

### Assumptions

- **High:** Increased staffing will improve safety and data quality.
- **Medium:** Staffing costs can be managed within budget constraints.

### SMART Validation Objective

By Month 24, achieve a minimum of 80% event capture rate with a response time to incidents of less than 5 minutes.

### Notes

- Monitor for potential budget impacts due to staffing increases.

### Expert Validation Steps

### Responsible Parties

- Principal Investigator

- **Medium:** Participants will feel safe and satisfied with the safety measures in place.

### Notes
