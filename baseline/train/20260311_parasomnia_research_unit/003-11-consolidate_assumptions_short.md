# Identify Risks
# Risk 1 - Regulatory & Permitting

- Delays or denial of permits for renovation/operation.
- Impact: 2-6 month delay, €10K-€50K cost increase, or abandonment.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: High
- Action: Engage authorities early, allocate budget/time, secure preliminary ethical approval.

# Risk 2 - Technical

- Failure of data acquisition model to capture parasomnia events.
- Impact: Reduced event capture, 6-12 month delay, re-evaluation of sensors.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Pilot testing, feedback loop, consider more PSG sessions.

# Risk 3 - Financial

- Cost overruns in renovation, equipment, or staffing.
- Impact: Budget shortfall, scope reduction, 3-9 month delay.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: High
- Action: Detailed estimates, contingency funds (10-15%), negotiate contracts, budget monitoring, explore funding.

# Risk 4 - Environmental

- Discovery of hazardous materials during renovation.
- Impact: 1-3 month delay, €5K-€20K remediation costs, health risks.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Environmental assessment, remediation plan, worker training.

# Risk 5 - Social

- Negative community reaction to the facility.
- Impact: Project delays, increased security costs, reputational damage, 1-4 week delay.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Community engagement, noise reduction, privacy, communication channel.

# Risk 6 - Operational

- High false alarm rate from safety systems.
- Impact: Reduced technician effectiveness, stress, safety breaches, 1-2 week delay.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Optimize sensitivity, clear protocols, regular training, adjust thresholds.

# Risk 7 - Supply Chain

- Delays in equipment delivery or unavailability.
- Impact: 2-4 week delay, increased costs.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Order early, multiple suppliers, backup equipment.

# Risk 8 - Security

- Data breach or loss of participant data.
- Impact: Legal penalties, reputational damage, loss of trust, 4-8 week delay.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: High
- Action: Data security measures, staff training, breach response plan.

# Risk 9 - Participant Recruitment & Retention

- Difficulty recruiting and retaining participants.
- Impact: Reduced sample size, protocol modifications, 6-12 month delay.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: High
- Action: Refine strategies, offer incentives, clear information, address concerns. 'Builder's Foundation' mitigates.

# Risk 10 - Data Annotation Workflow

- Low inter-rater reliability in event scoring.
- Impact: Reduced data quality, 2-4 week delay.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Training program, scoring criteria, dual-rater system, monitor reliability. 'Builder's Foundation' mitigates.

# Risk 11 - Staffing

- Difficulty hiring and retaining qualified staff.
- Impact: Staff shortages, compromised safety/quality, 1-3 week delay.
- Likelihood: Medium
- Severity: Medium
- Action: Competitive salaries, training, positive environment, flexible scheduling. 'Builder's Foundation' mitigates.

- Impact: Reduced funding, limited access to expertise.
- Likelihood: Low
- Severity: Low
- Action: Pursue partnership, compelling proposal, open communication.

## Issue 1 - Incomplete Financial Planning and Sensitivity Analysis
The budget allocation (50% Aim 1, 30% Aim 2, 20% Aim 3) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis. There's no cost breakdown within each aim. This makes the project vulnerable to cost overruns.

Recommendation:

- Develop a detailed work breakdown structure (WBS) for each aim.
- Create a comprehensive budget allocating funds to each task.
- Conduct a sensitivity analysis to assess the impact of potential cost increases. Consider a Monte Carlo simulation.
- Establish budget monitoring and control procedures.

Sensitivity:

- A 10% increase in renovation costs (€750,000 baseline) could reduce the budget for Aim 2 and Aim 3 by €75,000.
- A 20% increase in staffing costs (€1,140,000 baseline) could reduce the budget for Aims 2 and 3 by €228,000, potentially delaying completion by 3-6 months.

## Issue 2 - Lack of Specificity in Participant Recruitment and Retention Strategies
The plan lacks concrete strategies for participant retention. Participant dropout can impact sample size and statistical power. The plan needs to address barriers to participation.

Recommendation:

- Analyze potential barriers to participant recruitment and retention.
- Develop a detailed recruitment plan with outreach activities and eligibility criteria.
- Implement a retention plan with communication, incentives, and strategies for addressing concerns.
- Consider a mixed-methods approach to gather feedback.

Sensitivity:

- A 20% participant dropout rate (50 participants baseline) could reduce statistical power by 10-15% and delay publication by 2-4 months.
- Increasing participant compensation by 10% (€100 per participant-night baseline) could increase recruitment by 5-10%.

## Issue 3 - Insufficient Detail Regarding Data Privacy and Security Measures
The plan lacks details about data protection measures. Given the sensitive data, robust measures are essential for GDPR compliance. The plan needs to address data encryption, access controls, storage, and sharing protocols.

Recommendation:

- Conduct a data privacy risk assessment.
- Implement data encryption measures.
- Establish strict access controls.
- Develop a data breach response plan.
- Train staff on data privacy and security protocols.

Sensitivity:

- A data breach could result in fines (4% of annual turnover), reputational damage, and legal liabilities, potentially costing €100,000-€500,000 and delaying funding by 6-12 months.
- Implementing advanced data encryption and access control measures (€10,000 baseline) could reduce the risk of a data breach by 50-75%.

## Review conclusion
The project plan needs more detailed financial planning, robust participant strategies, and comprehensive data privacy measures. Addressing these issues will enhance the project's feasibility, ethical conduct, and scientific rigor.
