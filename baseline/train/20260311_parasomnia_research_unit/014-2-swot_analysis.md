
## Strengths 👍💪🦾
- Dedicated residential research unit allows for continuous, naturalistic observation of infrequent parasomnia events.
- Affiliation with University Hospital Bonn's Department of Epileptology provides access to expertise and resources.
- Tiered data acquisition model balances data richness with participant comfort.
- Explicit scientific program with clear aims and success criteria.
- Strong core team with diverse expertise (sleep medicine, neurophysiology, computational neuroscience, clinical psychology, data engineering).
- Gated budget ensures financial accountability and progress monitoring.
- External scientific advisory board provides independent oversight and guidance.
- Focus on methods validation and phenotyping work provides a solid foundation for future research.
- Data stored in EEG-BIDS-compatible format facilitates data sharing and collaboration.
- Builder's Foundation strategic path balances data acquisition, participant management, and operational efficiency.

## Weaknesses 👎😱🪫⚠️
- Reliance on infrequent parasomnia events makes event capture unpredictable.
- Residential setting introduces logistical and ethical complexities.
- Limited public data sharing due to privacy concerns restricts collaboration.
- Potential for high false alarm rates from safety systems can burden staff.
- Lack of a 'killer application' or immediately impactful deliverable beyond research findings.
- Budget allocation (50% Aim 1, 30% Aim 2, 20% Aim 3) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Lack of specificity in participant recruitment and retention strategies.
- Insufficient detail regarding data privacy and security measures.

## Opportunities 🌈🌐
- Develop semi-automated event-triage tools to reduce manual review burden and improve efficiency.
- Longitudinal data can reveal novel insights into parasomnia triggers and patterns.
- Potential for pharmaceutical industry partnership to expand research scope and funding.
- Establish the residential capture model as a gold standard for parasomnia research.
- Explore advanced signal processing techniques to enhance event detection and characterization.
- Develop a 'killer application' by creating a highly accurate, research-grade parasomnia event detection algorithm that can be integrated into clinical research tools or, with appropriate modifications and regulatory approvals, into future diagnostic devices. Obstacles include the need for extensive validation against human scorers, addressing edge cases and artifacts, and navigating regulatory pathways for clinical use.
- Leverage the collected data to develop personalized interventions for parasomnia management.
- Expand the research scope to include other sleep disorders or related neurological conditions.

## Threats ☠️🛑🚨☢︎💩☣︎
- Regulatory delays or denial of permits for renovation/operation.
- Cost overruns in renovation, equipment, or staffing.
- Negative community reaction to the facility.
- Difficulty recruiting and retaining participants.
- Low inter-rater reliability in event scoring.
- Difficulty hiring and retaining qualified staff.
- Data breach or loss of participant data.
- Ethical concerns related to participant safety and privacy.
- Competition from other sleep research centers.
- Changes in funding priorities or research regulations.

## Strategic Objectives 🎯🔭⛳🏅
- Achieve safe residential monitoring operations sustained for at least 24 months, as evidenced by zero serious safety incidents reported to the external scientific advisory board by 2028-Mar-11.
- Enroll 50-70 NREM parasomnia participants plus matched controls by 2028-Mar-11, as tracked by the study coordinator and verified through participant records.
- Capture a prespecified minimum number of 100 independently adjudicated parasomnia events sufficient for Aim 2 phenotyping analysis by 2028-Mar-11, as determined by dual human scoring and documented in the data annotation system.
- Publish 2-3 peer-reviewed publications in sleep medicine journals covering methods validation and longitudinal characterization by 2029-Mar-11, as evidenced by accepted manuscripts in reputable journals.
- Develop a benchmarked semi-automated event-triage model with reported sensitivity, specificity, and agreement against dual human scoring by 2029-Mar-11, as demonstrated through a validation study and documented in a technical report.
