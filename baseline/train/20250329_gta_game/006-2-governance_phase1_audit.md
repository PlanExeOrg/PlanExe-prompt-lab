
## Audit - Corruption Risks

- Bribery of government officials to expedite innovation grant approvals or reduce regulatory scrutiny.
- Kickbacks from suppliers of hardware/software in exchange for preferential treatment in procurement processes.
- Conflicts of interest arising from project managers or developers having undisclosed financial interests in strategic partner companies (e.g., automotive, fashion).
- Nepotism in hiring, favoring unqualified candidates who may be friends or relatives of project leaders, leading to inefficiencies and potential misuse of resources.
- Misuse of confidential project information (e.g., unreleased game features, strategic partnerships) for personal gain or to benefit competing studios.

## Audit - Misallocation Risks

- Inflated invoices from suppliers or contractors, with the excess funds being diverted for personal use.
- Double-billing for expenses, such as travel or entertainment, with both the project budget and personal funds being used.
- Inefficient allocation of resources, such as overspending on non-critical features or underfunding essential areas like security or testing.
- Unauthorized use of project assets, such as hardware or software, for personal projects or commercial ventures.
- Misreporting project progress or results to secure further funding or avoid scrutiny, leading to a false impression of the project's status.

## Audit - Procedures

- Conduct periodic internal audits of financial records, focusing on procurement processes, expense reports, and payroll, at least quarterly.
- Implement a robust contract review process with pre-defined approval thresholds for all vendor agreements, especially those involving strategic partnerships.
- Perform regular compliance checks to ensure adherence to ESRB, PEGI, GDPR, and CCPA regulations, documented and reviewed annually.
- Establish a whistleblower mechanism with clear reporting channels and protection against retaliation, reviewed and tested annually.
- Conduct a post-project external audit to assess the overall financial management and compliance of the project, including a review of government innovation grant usage.

## Audit - Transparency Measures

- Create a project progress dashboard accessible to key stakeholders (publisher, investors) showing key milestones, budget burn-down, and risk status.
- Publish minutes of key project governance meetings (e.g., steering committee, risk management board) on a secure internal portal.
- Document and publish the selection criteria for major vendors and strategic partners, including the rationale for the final decision.
- Make the project's data security and privacy plan publicly available (with appropriate redactions to protect sensitive information).
- Establish a clear and accessible policy on conflicts of interest, requiring all project personnel to disclose any potential conflicts and recuse themselves from relevant decisions.
