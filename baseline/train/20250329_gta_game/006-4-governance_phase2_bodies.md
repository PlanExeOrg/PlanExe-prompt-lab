### 1. Project Steering Committee

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Provides high-level strategic direction and oversight for the project, given its complexity, high budget ($500M), and strategic importance to the organization. Ensures alignment with overall business objectives and manages strategic risks.

**Responsibilities:**

- Approve overall project strategy and roadmap.
- Approve major project milestones and deliverables.
- Approve budget allocations exceeding $10 million.
- Monitor and manage strategic risks, including financial, technical, and reputational risks.
- Resolve strategic conflicts and escalate issues as needed.
- Oversee compliance with ethical guidelines and regulatory requirements.
- Approve key strategic partnerships.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Finalize Terms of Reference and operating procedures.
- Appoint a chairperson.
- Establish a communication plan.
- Define escalation paths and conflict resolution mechanisms.
- Review and approve the initial project plan.

**Membership:**

- Executive Sponsor (e.g., CEO or equivalent)
- Chief Technology Officer
- Chief Financial Officer
- Head of Game Development
- Independent External Advisor (Game Industry Expert)

**Decision Rights:** Strategic decisions related to project scope, budget (above $10 million), timeline, and key partnerships. Approval of major changes to the project plan.

**Decision Mechanism:** Decisions are made by majority vote, with the Executive Sponsor having the tie-breaking vote. Any decision with a significant ethical or compliance implication requires unanimous approval.

**Meeting Cadence:** Monthly

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Review of project progress against milestones.
- Discussion and approval of budget requests exceeding $10 million.
- Review and management of strategic risks.
- Updates on key partnerships and stakeholder engagement.
- Review of compliance reports and ethical considerations.
- Discussion of any escalated issues from other governance bodies.

**Escalation Path:** To the Board of Directors for issues exceeding the committee's authority or unresolved conflicts.
### 2. Project Management Office (PMO)

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Manages day-to-day execution of the project, ensuring adherence to timelines, budgets, and quality standards. Provides operational risk management and support to the project team.

**Responsibilities:**

- Develop and maintain the project plan, including detailed schedules and resource allocations.
- Monitor project progress and identify potential delays or cost overruns.
- Manage project budgets up to $10 million.
- Coordinate communication and collaboration among project team members.
- Implement and enforce project management methodologies and standards.
- Manage operational risks and escalate issues to the Project Steering Committee as needed.
- Track and report on project performance metrics.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Establish project management methodologies and standards.
- Develop a project communication plan.
- Set up project tracking and reporting systems.
- Recruit and train project management staff.
- Define roles and responsibilities within the PMO.

**Membership:**

- Project Manager
- Lead Programmers
- Lead Artists
- Lead Designers
- Project Coordinators
- Risk Manager

**Decision Rights:** Operational decisions related to project execution, resource allocation (within budget limits of $10 million), and risk management. Approval of minor changes to the project plan.

**Decision Mechanism:** Decisions are made by the Project Manager, in consultation with the PMO team. Disagreements are resolved through discussion and consensus, with the Project Manager having the final say within their delegated authority.

**Meeting Cadence:** Weekly

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Review of project progress against the plan.
- Discussion of potential risks and issues.
- Allocation of resources and management of budgets.
- Coordination of tasks and activities.
- Reporting on project performance metrics.
- Review of action items from previous meetings.

**Escalation Path:** To the Project Steering Committee for issues exceeding the PMO's authority or unresolved conflicts.
### 3. Technical Advisory Group

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Provides specialized technical expertise and guidance on key project aspects, such as procedural generation, AI integration, and performance optimization. Ensures the project leverages cutting-edge technologies effectively and mitigates technical risks.

**Responsibilities:**

- Evaluate and recommend technical solutions for key project challenges.
- Provide guidance on the integration of procedural generation, AI, and other advanced technologies.
- Review and approve technical designs and specifications.
- Conduct performance testing and optimization.
- Identify and mitigate technical risks.
- Stay abreast of emerging technologies and trends in the gaming industry.
- Advise on data security best practices.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Define the scope of the group's technical expertise.
- Identify and recruit technical experts.
- Establish a communication plan.
- Define processes for reviewing and approving technical designs.
- Set up a knowledge repository for sharing technical information.

**Membership:**

- Lead Programmer
- AI Specialist
- Procedural Generation Expert
- Graphics Engine Specialist
- Data Security Architect
- Independent External Technical Consultant

**Decision Rights:** Technical decisions related to technology selection, architecture, and implementation. Approval of technical designs and specifications.

**Decision Mechanism:** Decisions are made by consensus among the technical experts. In cases where consensus cannot be reached, the Lead Programmer has the final say, subject to review by the Project Steering Committee if the decision has strategic implications.

**Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Review of technical designs and specifications.
- Discussion of technical challenges and potential solutions.
- Evaluation of new technologies and trends.
- Performance testing and optimization results.
- Identification and mitigation of technical risks.
- Updates on data security measures.

**Escalation Path:** To the Project Steering Committee for issues exceeding the group's authority or unresolved technical conflicts.
### 4. Ethics & Compliance Committee

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Ensures the project adheres to ethical standards, regulatory requirements (ESRB, PEGI, GDPR, CCPA), and internal policies. Mitigates reputational and legal risks associated with controversial content, data privacy violations, and other ethical concerns.

**Responsibilities:**

- Develop and maintain a code of ethics for the project.
- Review and approve content to ensure compliance with ethical standards and regulatory requirements.
- Oversee the implementation of GDPR and CCPA compliance plans.
- Conduct regular audits to assess compliance with ethical and regulatory requirements.
- Investigate and resolve ethical complaints and compliance violations.
- Provide training on ethical standards and regulatory requirements.
- Monitor and address potential conflicts of interest.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Develop a code of ethics for the project.
- Establish a compliance plan for GDPR and CCPA.
- Define processes for reviewing and approving content.
- Set up a reporting mechanism for ethical complaints and compliance violations.
- Recruit and train committee members.

**Membership:**

- Legal Counsel
- Compliance Officer
- Human Resources Representative
- Content Review Specialist
- Data Protection Officer
- Independent External Ethics Advisor

**Decision Rights:** Decisions related to ethical standards, regulatory compliance, and content approval. Approval of the code of ethics and compliance plans.

**Decision Mechanism:** Decisions are made by majority vote, with the Legal Counsel having the tie-breaking vote. Any decision with a significant ethical or compliance implication requires unanimous approval.

**Meeting Cadence:** Monthly

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Review of content for ethical and regulatory compliance.
- Discussion of ethical complaints and compliance violations.
- Updates on GDPR and CCPA compliance plans.
- Review of audit results.
- Training on ethical standards and regulatory requirements.
- Discussion of potential conflicts of interest.

**Escalation Path:** To the Project Steering Committee for issues exceeding the committee's authority or unresolved ethical or compliance concerns.
### 5. Stakeholder Engagement Group

**Rationale for Inclusion:** Facilitates communication and collaboration with key stakeholders, including players, investors, strategic partners, and regulatory bodies. Ensures stakeholder feedback is incorporated into the project and manages stakeholder expectations.

**Responsibilities:**

- Develop and implement a stakeholder engagement plan.
- Conduct regular community updates and developer diaries.
- Gather feedback from players through early access programs and surveys.
- Collaborate with strategic partners for in-game content integration and cross-promotional opportunities.
- Engage with regulatory bodies to ensure compliance with content standards.
- Manage stakeholder expectations and address concerns.
- Monitor social media and online forums for stakeholder feedback.

**Initial Setup Actions:**

- Identify key stakeholders.
- Develop a stakeholder engagement plan.
- Establish communication channels with stakeholders.
- Set up a system for gathering and analyzing stakeholder feedback.
- Recruit and train group members.

**Membership:**

- Community Manager
- Public Relations Representative
- Marketing Representative
- Investor Relations Representative
- Strategic Partnerships Manager
- Player Representative (selected from the community)

**Decision Rights:** Decisions related to stakeholder engagement strategies, communication plans, and feedback mechanisms. Approval of stakeholder engagement plans.

**Decision Mechanism:** Decisions are made by consensus among the group members. In cases where consensus cannot be reached, the Community Manager has the final say, subject to review by the Project Steering Committee if the decision has strategic implications.

**Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly

**Typical Agenda Items:**

- Review of stakeholder feedback.
- Discussion of stakeholder engagement strategies.
- Planning of community updates and developer diaries.
- Coordination with strategic partners.
- Engagement with regulatory bodies.
- Management of stakeholder expectations.

**Escalation Path:** To the Project Steering Committee for issues exceeding the group's authority or unresolved stakeholder concerns.
