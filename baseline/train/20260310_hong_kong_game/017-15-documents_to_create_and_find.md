# Documents to Create

## Create Document 1: Project Charter

**ID**: 6c4bf95d-a4fe-4e86-b812-6ed431a83e49

**Description**: Formal document authorizing the project, defining its objectives, scope, and stakeholders. Includes high-level budget, timeline, and success criteria. Serves as the foundation for all subsequent planning.

**Responsible Role Type**: Project Manager

**Primary Template**: PMI Project Charter Template

**Secondary Template**: None

**Steps to Create**:

- Define project objectives and scope.
- Identify key stakeholders.
- Establish high-level budget and timeline.
- Define success criteria.
- Obtain stakeholder sign-off.

**Approval Authorities**: Executive Producer, Investors

**Essential Information**:

- What are the specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) objectives of the 'The Game' remake project?
- What is the detailed scope of the project, including in-scope and out-of-scope elements?
- Identify all key stakeholders (primary and secondary) and their roles and responsibilities.
- What is the high-level budget for the project, broken down into major categories (e.g., production, post-production, marketing)?
- What is the high-level timeline for the project, including key milestones and deadlines?
- Define the success criteria for the project, both qualitative and quantitative (e.g., box office revenue, critical acclaim, streaming deals).
- What are the key dependencies for the project (e.g., securing IP rights, casting key talent)?
- Identify the major risks associated with the project and the corresponding mitigation strategies.
- What are the regulatory and compliance requirements for filming in Hong Kong?
- What are the assumptions underlying the project plan, and what are the potential impacts if these assumptions prove to be incorrect?
- Detail the project's goal statement, ensuring it aligns with the overall strategic objectives.
- List the resources required for the project, including budget, personnel, equipment, and locations.
- What are the related goals that support the overall project objective?
- What are the relevant tags or keywords associated with the project for easy identification and categorization?
- What is the 'no smoking' policy on screen and how will it be enforced?

**Risks of Poor Quality**:

- An unclear scope definition leads to significant rework, scope creep, and budget overruns.
- Failure to identify key stakeholders results in miscommunication, conflicting priorities, and lack of buy-in.
- An unrealistic budget or timeline leads to project delays, reduced quality, and potential abandonment.
- Unclear success criteria make it difficult to measure project performance and determine whether the project has achieved its objectives.
- Inadequate risk assessment and mitigation strategies result in unforeseen problems and project delays.
- Missing regulatory and compliance requirements lead to legal issues, fines, and project delays.
- Unrealistic assumptions lead to poor decision-making and project failure.
- Lack of a clear goal statement results in a lack of focus and direction for the project team.

**Worst Case Scenario**: The project fails to secure IP rights, leading to legal action and project cancellation, resulting in a significant financial loss and reputational damage.

**Best Case Scenario**: The Project Charter clearly defines the project's objectives, scope, and stakeholders, enabling efficient planning, effective execution, and successful achievement of project goals, resulting in a commercially successful and critically acclaimed film.

**Fallback Alternative Approaches**:

- Utilize a pre-approved company template and adapt it to the specific requirements of the 'The Game' remake project.
- Schedule a focused workshop with key stakeholders to collaboratively define the project's objectives, scope, and success criteria.
- Engage a project management consultant or subject matter expert for assistance in developing the Project Charter.
- Develop a simplified 'minimum viable Project Charter' covering only the critical elements initially, and then expand it as more information becomes available.

## Create Document 2: Risk Register

**ID**: b9f3674c-16b7-4f54-bdde-455798f921e5

**Description**: A comprehensive log of potential risks, their likelihood, impact, and mitigation strategies.  Regularly updated throughout the project lifecycle.  In this case, specifically tailored to film production in Hong Kong.

**Responsible Role Type**: Risk Manager

**Primary Template**: Project Risk Register Template

**Secondary Template**: None

**Steps to Create**:

- Identify potential risks (regulatory, financial, technical, social, etc.).
- Assess the likelihood and impact of each risk.
- Develop mitigation strategies for each risk.
- Assign responsibility for monitoring and managing each risk.
- Regularly review and update the risk register.

**Approval Authorities**: Project Manager, Executive Producer

**Essential Information**:

- Identify all potential risks specific to the Hong Kong film production context, categorized by type (e.g., regulatory, financial, technical, social, environmental, operational, supply chain, security, talent availability, market/competition, long-term sustainability, narrative execution, currency fluctuation).
- For each identified risk, quantify the likelihood of occurrence (e.g., Low, Medium, High) and the potential impact on the project (e.g., cost increase, schedule delay, quality reduction, reputational damage), expressed in both qualitative and quantitative terms (e.g., HKD, weeks).
- Detail specific mitigation strategies for each risk, including proactive measures to reduce likelihood and reactive measures to minimize impact if the risk occurs.  Include responsible parties for each mitigation action.
- Define clear triggers or warning signs that indicate a risk is becoming more likely or is about to occur.
- Establish a risk scoring system (e.g., Likelihood x Impact) to prioritize risks and focus mitigation efforts on the most critical items.
- Include a section on residual risk – the risk that remains even after mitigation strategies are implemented.
- Specify the frequency of risk register review and update (e.g., weekly, monthly) and the process for escalating critical risks to project leadership.
- Requires access to the project plan, budget, timeline, stakeholder analysis, regulatory and compliance requirements, and assumptions documents.
- Requires input from key project personnel, including the director, producer, cinematographer, and legal counsel.

**Risks of Poor Quality**:

- Failure to identify critical risks leads to unexpected project disruptions, cost overruns, and schedule delays.
- Inaccurate risk assessment results in misallocation of resources and ineffective mitigation strategies.
- Outdated or incomplete risk information prevents proactive risk management and increases the likelihood of negative impacts.
- Lack of clear mitigation strategies leaves the project vulnerable to unforeseen events.
- Poorly defined risk ownership leads to inaction and delayed response to emerging threats.

**Worst Case Scenario**: A major, unmitigated risk (e.g., denial of filming permits due to political sensitivities, loss of key talent, or a significant currency fluctuation) forces project abandonment after substantial investment, resulting in significant financial loss and reputational damage.

**Best Case Scenario**: The Risk Register enables proactive identification and mitigation of potential problems, leading to smooth project execution, adherence to budget and schedule, and successful delivery of a high-quality film. It also provides a clear audit trail of risk management activities, demonstrating due diligence to investors and stakeholders.

**Fallback Alternative Approaches**:

- Utilize a simplified risk assessment matrix focusing only on high-impact risks initially.
- Conduct a brainstorming session with the core project team to identify potential risks and mitigation strategies.
- Adapt a generic film production risk register template to the specific context of the Hong Kong project.
- Engage a risk management consultant for a focused risk assessment workshop.
