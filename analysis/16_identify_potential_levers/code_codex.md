- **I1 — Salvage levers individually instead of rejecting the whole batch.** The most impactful change is to stop treating `DocumentDetails.levers` as an all-or-nothing unit. Even a simple fallback that keeps valid levers and drops only the malformed entries would convert run-23-style total failures into partial successes.

## Summary

The biggest code-level issues are not about idea quality; they are about contract design. The step currently mixes contradictory prompt/schema instructions, overlong/partly unused output requirements, and all-or-nothing validation. That combination explains the observed pattern well: weaker models slip through with shallow boilerplate, near-miss but useful outputs get discarded entirely, and verbose plans/models hit truncation or timeout. The highest-value fixes are to slim the response contract, salvage per-lever near misses, and stop using name-only novelty/dedup rules.
